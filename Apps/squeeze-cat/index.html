How to Kegel

1

Find Your Muscles

Imagine stopping the flow of urine mid-stream, or preventing passing gas. That's your pelvic floor engaging.

2

Get Comfortable

Sit, stand, or lie down. Many find it easiest lying on their back with knees bent.

3

Squeeze

Exhale and gently contract your pelvic floor. Lift inward and upward, like an elevator going up.

4

Hold & Breathe

Hold the contraction while breathing normally. Start with 3-5 seconds.

5

Release

Inhale and completely release. Let the pelvic floor soften fully. This is just as important as the squeeze!

Tips for Success

  • Quality over quantity - focus on form
  • Keep glutes, abs, and thighs relaxed
  • Practice daily for best results
  • Results typically appear in 4-6 weeks
